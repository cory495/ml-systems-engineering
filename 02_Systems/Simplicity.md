# Simplicity

Date: 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Definition

---
Simplicity is to solve problems in the most basic ways possible so as to avoid complexity.
## 2. Why It Matters

---
Reducing complexity ensured that maintenance remains easy.
## 3. Key Ideas

---
- Less is more.
- Keep your eyes open for good abstractions that can allow large systems to be broken into well-defined, reusable components.
## 4. Examples

---
Software mired in complexity (a big ball of mud) can cause explosion of the state space, tight coupling of modules, tangled dependencies, inconsistent naming and terminology, hacks aimed at solving performance problems, special-casting, and much more.
## 5. How It Is Achieved

---
There are many different ways to achieve simplicity. For instance, high-level programming languages attempt to do this.
## 6. Tradeoffs

---
Simplicity can cost performance if taken to the extreme.

## 8. Summary
Simplicity is the attempt to reduce complexity as much as possible.
