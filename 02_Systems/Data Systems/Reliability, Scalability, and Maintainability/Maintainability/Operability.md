# Operability

Date: 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Definition

---
Operability is making it easy for operations teams to keep systems running smoothly.
## 2. Why It Matters

---
This is important because it can save on personnel cost and operations cost.
## 3. Key Ideas

---
- Operations teams are vital for maintainability.
## 4. Examples

---
- Several software engineers often work on different systems to ensure they stay running over time. Typically, there are things like backend dashboards for them to ensure everything is running smoothly.
## 5. How It Is Achieved

---
- Provide visibility for runtime behavior and internals with good monitoring.
- Good support for automation and integration.
- Avoid dependency on individual machines.
- Good documentation and simple operational models.
- Good default behavior.
- Self-healing systems.
- Predictable behavior.
## 6. Tradeoffs

---
- Operability takes time to set up but saves time in the long-term.

Operability is necessary for operations teams to be able to maintain systems over time.
