# Software Errors

Date: 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Definition

---
Software errors are faults that occur in the software rather than in hardware.
## 2. Why It Matters

---
Unlike hardware faults, software faults can quickly escalate in a chain reaction.
## 3. Key Ideas

---
- Typically, these are bugs.
- They can runaway and cause chain reactions.
## 4. Examples

---
- A software bug that causes every instance to crash.
- A runaway process that uses up a shared resource.
- a service that the system depends on slows down, becomes unresponsive, or returns corrupted responses.
- Cascading failures where one software error causes another one and another one.
## 5. How It Is Achieved

---
- Software errors are avoided through procedures and testing.
## 6. Tradeoffs

---
- Avoiding software errors can reduce the opportunity costs caused by downtime but also costs money upfront.
