# Human Errors

Date: 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Definition

---
Human errors are errors caused by either the operators or users of the system. Humans are unreliable and the leading cause of failures.
## 2. Why It Matters

---
Human errors by far cause the most faults, thus they must be given special attention so as to avoid failures.
## 3. Key Ideas

---
- Humans are unreliable.
- Systems should be built around human unreliability.
## 4. Examples

---
- A human trying to go to a webpage that does not exist. There is no 404 page, so the program hangs.
## 5. How It Is Achieved

---
- Human errors can be avoided by minimizing opportunities for error.
- Decoupling places where people make the most mistakes.
- Test every level thoroughly.
- Allow quick and easy recovery from human errors.
- Set up detailed and clear monitoring. This is often referred to as telemetry.
- Have good management practices and trainings.
## 6. Tradeoffs

---
- Avoiding human errors can take time and money.
