# Reliability

Date: 2026-06-05

---

## 1. Definition

---
The system should work correctly in the face of hardware faults, software faults, and human error.
- The application performs the desired function.
- The application tolerates user errors or software glitches.
- The application prevents unauthorized access and abuse
## 2. Why It Matters

---
Reliability matters because users expect a certain degree of reliability. Reliability is one mechanism for gaining the respect of your users.
## 3. Key Ideas

---
- Fault-tolerant systems are important to create.
- Reliability is wanted and expected by users.

## 4. Examples

---
Relational database management systems (RDBMS) are a key example of reliable systems. They incorporate atomicity, consistency, isolation, and durability.
## 5. How It Is Achieved

---
Reliability is achieved there multiple procedures and systems working together.
## 6. Tradeoffs

---
More reliability typically involves higher cost.
