# Data Systems

Date: 2026-06-05

---
![[pic_systems_datasystem_example.png]]
## 1. Problem
Data systems solve the problem of storing, organizing, processing, and analyzing large amounts of data. Three main problems data systems solve are [[reliability]], [[scalability]], and [[maintainability]].

---

## 2. Intuition
Data systems are collections of tools and processes for storage, management, and retrieval of information. They are like digital libraries optimized for storing information and looking quickly through them to find what you need.

---

## 3. How It Works
- Step 1: The hardware that goes into the system (GPUs, CPUs, Hard drives, etc.).
- Step 2: The software that goes into the system (SQL Databases, MongoDB, queues, stacks, heaps, etc.).
- Step 3: The humans that maintain and use the system.

---

## 4. Key Components
- Hardware, software, data, procedures, users, and developers are all key parts of data systems.

---

## 5. Tradeoffs
Different data systems have different pros and cons for use.

---

## 6. Scaling / Complexity
Time, space, and bottlenecks are based on the specific data system in question.

## 7. Real Systems Usage
Where this shows up in industry:

- Websites

---

## 8. Failure Modes
- Hardware breaks, typically because of wear and tear.
- Software breaks, typically because of errors in code.
- More errors can occur from human error, when the software is used in a way that is unexpected.
